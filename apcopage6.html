Saskatchewan Saskatchewan

National Sport

In 2001, Saskatchewans official sport was announced as Curling. Most people in Saskatchewan have considered it to be so for many years. It is also referred to as the roaring game because of the loud noise created by the brooms used to sweep rocks and such off the ice, curling has a large history in the Saskatchewan. The fabled Richardson brothers won four Canadian and World men's championships between. Another great rink from Regina, the Sandra Schmirler team, won three Canadian and World womens championships in the, followed by the first ever womens Olympic gold medal in curling at 1998.
