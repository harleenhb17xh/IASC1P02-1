The official flag of Saskatchewan's was created in1969. It consists of the shield of arms, in addition, the floral emblem, which is a western red lily. The flag's top half is green in color, which represents the forest in the north of Saskatchewan; the bottom half is a golden color, which represents the grains in the south of Saskatchewan.

In 1941, the flower, the chosen official flower of Saskatchewan was the red lily. In moist green areas and wooded areas, this flower is found. Due to its red bright color, it stands out apart from the majority of the natural green.
